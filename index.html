A read on where AI fits in DeckDive's scoring pipeline, and where deterministic code does the work better.

You built a platform that reads pitch decks and ranks them against a fund's thesis. Here is the layer that sits above the model.

The hypothesis

You built a platform that reads pitch decks and ranks them against a fund's thesis. Credit Agricole and Vertis SGR trust the output enough to put their names on your site. That earns a next question from every serious buyer after them: how does the scoring stay consistent when one fund rewrites its thesis, another adds a sector filter, and a third asks where a specific signal came from.

Most AI products answer that question with more model. The layer that actually solves it sits above the model. We call it computational orchestration.

The 60 / 30 / 10 read

A system like yours, sorted by layer.

Teams who skip this distinction ship LLM calls where Postgres would finish the job faster, cheaper, and with an audit trail a bank lawyer can read.

60%
Traditional code

Ingestion, parsing, structured extraction, benchmark lookups, dashboard state.

30%
Rule-based logic

Fund-specific filters, compliance guardrails, scoring weights, override rules.

10%
Genuine AI

The semantic read on a deck, the open-ended Q&A, the narrative summary.

A workshop, a set of advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. That is the shape of the first engagement.

  • Workshop to map the real AI, code, and rule boundaries inside the product.
  • Advisory calls to pressure-test the architecture against live deals.
  • Organizational Context Architecture delivered as a layered spec.
  • Strategic Operations Framework the team runs after we hand it over.
The published method

ICM: Interpretable Context Methodology.

Agent context organized as a layered filesystem that a small team can maintain and a compliance reviewer can audit. For a scoring product with investor-specific criteria and a bank partner, it is the remedy for prompt drift and review-cycle pain.
